SubDomain TakeOver

What is Subdomain Takeover?

A subdomain takeover occurs when an attacker gains control of an unused or unclaimed subdomain, often due to misconfigurations in DNS settings. This can lead to phishing, spreading malware, or damaging the organization's reputation.

How Does It Happen?

Common reasons for subdomain takeovers include:

  • Pointing a DNS entry to a decommissioned cloud service.
  • Forgetting to delete DNS records after removing a service.
  • Not monitoring DNS configurations regularly.

How to Protect Against It?

Follow these steps to mitigate the risk:

  1. Audit and monitor your DNS records regularly.
  2. Remove unused DNS entries.
  3. Verify the ownership of subdomains periodically.
  4. Use tools to identify vulnerable subdomains.
